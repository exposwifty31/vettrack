Here is your idea translated into a clean, execution-ready prompt:

⸻

Prompt (Demo Video Creation):

You are a product marketing and UX expert.

Your task is to design a short demo video (60–90 seconds) that showcases how the app (VetTrack) is used in a real veterinary hospital environment.

Do NOT just list features.
Build a realistic usage flow that tells a clear story.

⸻

Goal

Show how the app solves real problems in a veterinary hospital:
	•	Equipment getting lost
	•	Lack of accountability
	•	Poor communication between staff

The video should feel like a real shift scenario, not a feature walkthrough.

⸻

Structure

1. Opening (5–7 seconds)

Show the problem:
	•	“Where is the equipment?”
	•	“No one knows who took it”

Keep it short and clear.

⸻

2. Scan → Check-Out
	•	User scans equipment
	•	Taps “Check Out”

Overlay text:

“Scan. Assign. Done.”

⸻

3. Real Usage Context
	•	Equipment is being used (e.g. IV pump / monitor)
	•	Optionally assigned to a room or patient

⸻

4. Issue Reporting
	•	Scan equipment
	•	Tap “Report Issue”
	•	Add note + photo

Overlay text:

“Clear issue reporting. No guesswork.”

⸻

5. Alerts
	•	Alert appears
	•	User taps “I’m handling this”

Overlay text:

“No missed problems. No duplicates.”

⸻

6. My Equipment
	•	Show user’s assigned equipment list

Overlay text:

“Full accountability per shift”

⸻

7. Return Flow
	•	Scan equipment
	•	Tap “Return”

Overlay text:

“Everything goes back”

⸻

8. Ending

Strong closing line:

Examples:
	•	“Know where every tool is.”
	•	“Nothing gets lost.”

⸻

Filming Guidelines
	•	Use real app screens (screen recording preferred)
	•	Simulate realistic data (equipment names, rooms, etc.)
	•	Show a continuous story (not random feature jumps)

⸻

Important
	•	Keep transitions fast
	•	No unnecessary steps
	•	Focus on clarity and real-world usage
	•	Every scene must answer: “Why does this matter?”

⸻

Output

Provide:
	1.	Full video script (scene-by-scene)
	2.	Suggested on-screen text
	3.	Optional voiceover script
	4.	Timing per section

⸻

Design this for:

A busy veterinary technician —
Everything must feel fast, obvious, and useful.
