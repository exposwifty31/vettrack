# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Veterinary hospital staff in Israel: vets, senior and junior technicians, admins, and students. They're mid-task — scanning equipment, dispensing medication, tracking inventory, managing hospitalizations. Hebrew is default; the interface is RTL. Users are time-pressured and expert; they don't have patience for a learning curve on each screen.

## Product Purpose

VetTrack is the operating system for a veterinary hospital. Equipment tracking, medication workflows, inventory, scheduling, billing, Code Blue coordination. Success means staff can complete clinical tasks without the software ever becoming the obstacle.

## Brand Personality

Clinical. Precise. Trusted.

The interface of a medical instrument, not a consumer app. Every element earns its place. Nothing decorative. Authority comes from clarity, not from chrome.

## Anti-references

- **Generic hospital software**: heavy blues, institutional grays, forms that feel like 2005 EMR systems. Bureaucratic, not clinical.
- **Consumer wellness apps**: pastel purples, rounded blobs, friendly illustrations, emoji-heavy tone. This is a professional tool.
- **Dark mode SaaS**: dark backgrounds with neon accents — looks like a dev tool, not a clinical environment.
- **Generic Tailwind template**: identical card grids, uniform padding, shadcn defaults straight out of the box. No identity.

## Design Principles

1. **The tool disappears into the task.** Staff are executing clinical workflows under time pressure. The UI surfaces what they need and gets out of the way. Nothing decorative; no cognitive overhead.
2. **Trust through consistency.** Medical tools behave predictably. Every button shape, form control, and state indicator must follow the same vocabulary screen to screen. Surprise is a defect.
3. **Hierarchy over decoration.** Information priority drives visual weight — not color, not animation. What matters most must be visible in under 200ms.
4. **Hebrew-first, visually native.** RTL is not an afterthought. Labels, spacing, icon placement, and reading order are designed for Hebrew. English should feel like a translation, not the original.
5. **Earned familiarity.** The interface should feel immediately learnable to anyone who knows Linear, Stripe, or Notion. Use the standard affordances well, not reinvented ones badly.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. RTL/Hebrew layout native. Reduced motion support required (clinical environments may include staff with motion sensitivities). Sufficient contrast for bright or variable clinic lighting.
