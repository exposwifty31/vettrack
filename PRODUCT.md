# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Veterinary hospital staff in Israel: vets, senior and junior technicians, admins, and students. They are mid-task — scanning equipment, checking out assets, completing tasks, restocking inventory, responding to Code Blue. Hebrew is default; the interface is RTL. Users are time-pressured and expert; the software must not become the obstacle.

## Product Purpose

VetTrack is the operating layer for hospital **equipment and floor operations**: asset lifecycle, waitlist, room radar, unified tasks, Code Blue coordination, inventory, and PMS integrations. Success means staff complete operational workflows without the software getting in the way.

## Brand Personality

Clinical. Precise. Trusted.

The interface of a medical instrument, not a consumer app. Every element earns its place. Nothing decorative. Authority comes from clarity, not from chrome.

## Anti-references

- **Generic hospital software**: heavy blues, institutional grays, forms that feel like 2005 EMR systems.
- **Consumer wellness apps**: pastel purples, rounded blobs, friendly illustrations.
- **Dark mode SaaS**: looks like a dev tool, not a clinical environment.
- **Generic Tailwind template**: identical card grids with no product identity.

## Design Principles

1. **The tool disappears into the task.** Surface what staff need under time pressure; no decorative chrome.
2. **Trust through consistency.** Predictable controls and states screen to screen.
3. **Hierarchy over decoration.** Information priority drives visual weight.
4. **Hebrew-first, visually native.** RTL is not an afterthought.
5. **Earned familiarity.** Standard affordances done well (Linear/Stripe/Notion-level clarity).

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. RTL/Hebrew layout native. Reduced motion support required. Sufficient contrast for variable clinic lighting.

## Out of scope (post–June 2026)

Full EMR, patient registry, medication formulary, pharmacy forecast, and ER intake board were removed from the product scope. Legacy URLs redirect to equipment/tasks surfaces — see `docs/scope-change-2026.md`.
